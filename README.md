# Excel Sales Dashboards — Portfolio Projects

Two end-to-end Excel dashboard projects built for a data analyst portfolio,
covering sales analysis across retail and e-commerce datasets.

---

## Project 1 — Coffee Sales Dashboard (2019–2022)

**Tools:** Excel, Pivot Tables, Slicers, Dynamic Charts, VLOOKUP

![Coffee Sales Dashboard](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0019da58-60dd-47b6-9e4e-c0d598d38547)

### What I did
- Analysed coffee sales data spanning 4 years and 3 countries, and built
  an interactive dashboard to track revenue trends, product performance,
  and customer behaviour
- Used pivot tables and slicers to allow filtering by date, roast type,
  and bag size — making it easy to explore the data without touching any
  formulas
- Found that the US accounted for nearly 80% of total revenue, while
  Ireland surprisingly outsold the UK despite being a smaller market
- Spotted a sharp revenue drop in Q3 2022 and traced it back through the
  trend line — the kind of thing that would easily get missed without a
  visual dashboard
- Looked at which customers were buying the most and whether they had
  loyalty cards — loyalty card holders consistently placed larger orders
- Broke down sales by coffee type and roast profile to see what was
  actually selling — Robusta and Liberica together made up more than half
  of all sales

### Key numbers
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total Revenue | ₹45,134 |
| Top Market (US) | ₹35,638 — 79% of total |
| Peak Quarter | Q3 2021 |
| Top Coffee Types | Robusta + Liberica → 54% of sales |
| Top Customer Revenue (Top 5) | ₹1,473 combined |

## Project 2 — Bicycle Shop Sales Dashboard (2021–2022)

**Tools:** Excel, Pivot Tables, Charts, Conditional Formatting, Slicers

![Bicycle Shop Sales Dashboard](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/10b22abb-b68b-46aa-a2b8-c93f4bb8a234)

### What I did
- Built a sales dashboard for a bicycle retail business covering 2 years
  of data across 8 countries, tracking revenue, profit, units sold, and
  customer-level performance
- Total sales came to $11.87M with a net profit of $1.69M — broke this
  down by month, product, country, and customer segment to make the
  numbers actually usable
- Found that the Government segment was driving 44% of revenue, which was
  not obvious from the raw data — Small Business came second at 36%,
  while Enterprise was underperforming relative to its potential
- Noticed that Paseo was the top revenue product at $3.3M even though it
  ranked fifth in units sold — suggesting a significantly higher price
  point than the other products
- One customer alone was responsible for $1.59M in sales, flagging a
  customer concentration risk worth addressing from a business continuity
  standpoint
- Added year and month slicers so anyone using the dashboard could slice
  the data by time period or region without needing to know how the file
  was built

### Key numbers
| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total Sales | $11.87M |
| Net Profit | $1.69M (14.23% margin) |
| Countries Covered | 8 |
| Top Segment | Government — 44% of revenue |
| Top Product by Revenue | Paseo — $3.3M |
| Top Customer Contribution | $1.59M (single customer) |
| Units Sold | 11.25 lakh |
